TheYHack is Brigham Young University's 24-hour hackathon event.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon can be thought of as an "invention marathon". Rather than breaking into computer systems, you start from scratch to create something new and innovative. You may form teams, or work on your own. For it to be eligible for prizes, your project must have been started during the Hackathon. Workshops will also be available on a variety of topics to learn about new technologies and subjects!

It's a great opportunity to get started on learning that new framework, try to change the world, or if you'd like, you can just work on homework and enjoy the hackathon atmosphere!

How do I sign up?

You can sign up by filling out the registration form here.

When and where is TheYHack?

TheYHack will be held March 29th and 30th, in the stepdown lounge of the Clyde Building on BYU campus.

Are there any rules or guidelines I should be aware of?

The MLH Code of Conduct can be found here.

Who can attend TheYHack?

Any and all students may attend, regardless of university or field of study.

Can I work on a team?

Of course! You may work on a team of up to four students.

What time does it start?

The event goes from 5pm on the 29th until 5pm on the 30th. We recommend you plan on staying overnight.

What should I bring?

  • Laptop, phone, and chargers
  • Music/headphones
  • Additional hardware for your project (ie, desktop computer, phone/tablet, raspberry pi, etc.)
  • A pencil/pen and paper
  • Toothbrush/toothpaste/deodorant/etc.
  • Sweater/layers of warmer clothing
  • Personal medications

How do I win?

The coolest project that was created entirely during the hackathon will win.

I'd like to teach a workshop

We'd love to have you! You can sign up to teach a workshop on the register page.

Where can I find information about sponsoring the event?

Contact us by email at cs.byu.acm@gmail.com

You can email us at cs.byu.acm@gmail.com
