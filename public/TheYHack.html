TheYHack is Brigham Young University's 24-hour hackathon event. Check back here later for more information!

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon can be thought of as an "invention marathon". Rather than breaking into computer systems, you start from scratch to create something new and innovative. You may form teams, or work on your own. For it to be eligible for prizes, your project must have been started during the Hackathon.

It's a great opportunity to get started on learning that new framework, try to change the world, or if you'd like, you can just work on homework and enjoy the hackathon atmosphere!

How do I sign up?

You can sign up by filling out the registration form here.

When and where is TheYHack?

TheYHack will be held March 23rd and 24th, in the stepdown lounge of the Clyde Building on BYU campus.

The MLH Code of Conduct can be found here.

Who can attend TheYHack?

Any and all students may attend, regardless of university or field of study.

Can I work on a team?

Of course! You may work on a team of up to four students.

What time does it start?

The event goes from 5pm on the 23rd until 5pm on the 24th. We recommend you plan on staying overnight.

What should I bring?

Whatever you need to be able to code all night! You'll probably want a sleeping bag or caffeine (or both)

How do I win?

The coolest project that was created entirely during the hackathon will win.
